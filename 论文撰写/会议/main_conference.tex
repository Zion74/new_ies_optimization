\begin{abstract}
Capacity planning of distributed combined cooling, heating and power (CCHP) systems is usually driven by annual cost, while source--load coordination remains implicit. Conventional matching surrogates first compress each carrier over time and then aggregate the resulting scalars, obscuring simultaneous mismatch of electricity, heat, and cold. We propose the \emph{Instantaneous Euclidean Matching Index} (IEMI), which treats the three net imbalances at each time step as a vector and uses its Euclidean norm as the magnitude of instantaneous multi-carrier mismatch. Coupled with annualized cost in a bi-objective NSGA-II framework, IEMI is compared with cost-only planning and a standard-deviation baseline on a German heating-dominated community benchmark. IEMI widens the reachable Pareto cost span from EUR~209k to EUR~1{,}267k. Under the same +100\% budget allowance relative to the economic optimum, the selected IEMI design reduces peak grid import by 26.7\% and annual grid purchase by 29.9\%, while increasing self-sufficiency from 38.6\% to 57.0\%. The gain appears mainly in the medium-to-high investment region rather than as uniform dominance at all budgets. The $L_2$ choice is used to represent vector magnitude, not to claim a universally harsher penalty than $L_1$ aggregation.
\end{abstract}
