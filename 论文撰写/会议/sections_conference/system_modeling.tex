We model a four-bus CCHP system with electricity, heating, cooling, and natural gas. Equipment includes PV, wind, gas turbine (CHP), electric heat pump, electric chiller, absorption chiller, and electric, thermal, and cold storages \cite{hilpert2018oemof,mancarella2014mes}. Component relations are linear with fixed efficiencies/COPs, so a candidate capacity vector is evaluated through a 24-hour economic dispatch subject to bus balances and bounds.

Annualized planning cost combines investment annuities, a demand charge on peak grid import, and operating cost. To keep the evolutionary inner loop tractable, the year is represented by 14 weighted typical days obtained from clustered load-weather profiles \cite{kotzur2018impact,dominguez2012selection}. Annual operating cost and the matching index are weighted sums over those days, while full-year 8760-hour simulation is used only after optimization to assess selected Pareto points.
