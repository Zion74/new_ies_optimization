We introduced the Instantaneous Euclidean Matching Index (IEMI) for CCHP capacity planning: a time average of the Euclidean norm of the three-carrier net-imbalance vector. On the German community case, IEMI widens the attainable planning trade-off range relative to a standard-deviation baseline and, under the same +100\% budget allowance, improves full-year peak import, annual grid purchase, and self-sufficiency. The gain appears mainly once medium-to-high investment levels become accessible. The $L_2$ choice reflects geometric magnitude of joint mismatch, not a claim of universally stronger penalization than $L_1$.

The evidence chain is explicit: typical-day optimization generates candidate designs, budget-normalized Pareto selection chooses comparable points, and 8760-hour re-dispatch confirms the operational consequences. The present note is intentionally limited to one heating-dominated benchmark with fixed carrier weights and benchmark-specific tariffs; broader robustness and sensitivity analysis are natural next steps for a journal extension.
